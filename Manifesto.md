## Executive Summary

This manifesto extends the **Cognitive Entropy Minimization Law (CEML)** into a broader, information-theoretic framework for physical reality: the **Unified Information Compression Theory (UICT)**.

**Core Claim:** Matter, energy, spacetime, and consciousness are emergent forms of information in different compression states. Systems — from neurons to galaxies — evolve by optimizing a coherence-vs-entropy tradeoff:

$$
\Psi^* = \underset{\Psi}{\mathrm{argmax}} \left(\frac{C(\Psi \mid \Omega)}{H(\Psi) + \epsilon}\right)
$$

Where $C(\Psi\mid\Omega)$ measures coherence (contextual alignment/compression) and $H(\Psi)$ measures entropic cost (complexity/disorder). This single optimization principle (CEML generalized) yields predictions across cosmology, gravity, chemistry, and consciousness.

## 1. Fundamental Postulates

### Axiom 1: Informational Monism
The universe is fundamentally informational. Physical entities (particles, fields, spacetime geometry, biological systems, minds) are configurations of information at different compression levels.

### Axiom 2: Universal Optimization (Generalized CEML)
Physical systems preferentially occupy states maximizing coherence per entropic cost:

$$
\Psi^* = \arg\max_\Psi \frac{C(\Psi|\Omega)}{H(\Psi) + \epsilon}
$$

* $\Omega$: Environmental/contextual constraints.
* $\epsilon$: A small regularizer preventing singularities for $H\to 0$.

### Axiom 3: Compression–Density Equivalence
Mass–energy density is (up to scaling) a function of algorithmic/Kolmogorov compression of the underlying information state:

$$
\rho(x) \propto \mathcal{K}(\Psi(x))
$$

**Implication:** Higher compression $\Rightarrow$ higher effective density/stability.

## 3. Mathematical Framework

### 3.1 Universal Information Field ($\Phi(x,t)$)
Introduce a field $\Phi : \mathcal{M}^4 \to \mathcal{H}$ mapping spacetime points to states in a (possibly very large) Hilbert space $\mathcal{H}$ of information configurations.

### 3.2 Density–Compression Relation
A phenomenological relation:

$$
\rho(x) = \rho_{\text{Planck}} \cdot \frac{\mathcal{C}(\Phi(x))^n}{1 - \mathcal{C}(\Phi(x))}
$$

With $n\ge2$ controlling steepness and $\rho_{\text{Planck}}$ as a scaling constant. $\mathcal{C}\in[0,1)$ is a normalized compression/coherence measure.

### 3.3 Generalized Statistical Weight
State stability is modeled by:

$$
P(\psi) \propto \exp\left(-\beta \cdot \frac{H(\psi)}{C(\psi|\Omega)}\right)
$$

Low $H$ and high $C$ exponentially favor occupancy.

### 3.4 Entropic Action Principle
Define an action functional where physical evolution arises from stationary action $\delta S/\delta \Phi = 0$:

$$
S[\Phi] = \int d^4x \left( \frac{C(\Phi|\partial\Phi)}{H(\Phi) + \epsilon} \right)
$$

This is an informational analogue to variational principles in physics.
