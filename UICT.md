## Executive Summary

The **Unified Information Compression Theory (UICT)** proposes that information is the fundamental substrate of reality, and that matter, energy, spacetime, chemistry, and consciousness are emergent phenomena corresponding to different compression states of the universal information field.

The core dynamical principle governing all systems is a generalized form of the **Cognitive Entropy Minimization Law (CEML)**:

$$
\Psi^* = \underset{\Psi}{\mathrm{argmax}} \left( \frac{C(\Psi|\Omega)}{H(\Psi) + \epsilon} \right)
$$

Where:

  * $C(\Psi|\Omega)$: Contextual coherence / compressibility
  * $H(\Psi)$: Entropy / informational complexity
  * $\epsilon$: Stability regulator

This principle describes structure formation, gravitation, molecular stability, biological evolution, cognition, and conscious experience. The UICT is not a replacement for physics but a more general generative principle from which known laws appear as special cases.

## 1\. Fundamental Postulates

### 1.1 Informational Monism

Everything that exists is a configuration of a universal information substrate. Matter and energy are derivative states.

### 1.2 Compression–Stability Principle (Generalized CEML)

Systems evolve toward states that maximize coherence per unit entropy. This governs physical evolution the same way free-energy minimization governs thermodynamics and biological adaptation:

$$
\Psi^* = \arg\max_\Psi \frac{C(\Psi|\Omega)}{H(\Psi)+\epsilon}
$$

### 1.3 Compression–Density Equivalence

Mass–energy density emerges from algorithmic compression. High compression implies high information density, perceived physically as mass:

$$
\rho(x) \propto \mathcal{K}(\Psi(x))
$$

Where $\mathcal{K}$ is a normalized Kolmogorov-compression proxy.

## 3\. Mathematical Framework

### 3.1 Universal Information Field $\Phi$

We define a field mapping spacetime to informational states in a large Hilbert space $\mathcal{H}$:

$$
\Phi : \mathcal{M}^4 \rightarrow \mathcal{H}
$$

### 3.2 Density–Compression Model

$$
\rho(x) = \rho_{\text{Planck}} \cdot \frac{\mathcal{C}(\Phi(x))^{n}}{1 - \mathcal{C}(\Phi(x))}
$$

With $n\ge 2$ tuning steepness; near maximal compression ($\mathcal{C}\to1$), density approaches infinity ($\rho \to \infty$).

### 3.3 Informational Statistical Weight

State probability is determined by the ratio of entropy to compression:

$$
P(\psi) \propto \exp\left(-\beta\cdot\frac{H(\psi)}{C(\psi|\Omega)}\right)
$$

States with **low entropy** and **high compression** are exponentially preferred.

### 3.4 Entropic Action Principle

Physical evolution is defined by stationary action on the informational manifold:

$$
S[\Phi] = \int d^4x \left( \frac{C(\Phi|\partial\Phi)}{H(\Phi)+\epsilon} \right) \implies \frac{\delta S}{\delta \Phi} = 0
$$

This generalizes classical and quantum variational principles.
